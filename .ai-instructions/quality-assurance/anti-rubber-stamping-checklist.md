# Anti-Rubber-Stamping Checklist

**Purpose:** Prevent same-agent bias in critic phases  
**Load:** Before submitting ANY critic analysis  
**Scope:** All operational modes, all stages

---

## ✅ MANDATORY PRE-SUBMISSION CHECKLIST

**Before submitting critic analysis, verify ALL of the following:**

- [ ] I have identified AT LEAST 2-3 issues or observations
- [ ] If zero issues found, I have written 200+ word justification explaining exceptional quality
- [ ] I have challenged at least 3 assumptions from the work phase
- [ ] I have proposed alternative interpretations or approaches
- [ ] My average score across dimensions is below 4.5/5.0
- [ ] I have identified edge cases or scenarios not covered in work phase
- [ ] I have questioned at least one confidence level as potentially too high
- [ ] I have found at least one documentation omission or gap
- [ ] If giving ANY 5/5 score, I have provided extraordinary justification
- [ ] I am being adversarial, not confirmatory

**If you cannot check ALL boxes above:**
- ❌ Your critic analysis is INCOMPLETE
- ❌ Return to analysis with heightened adversarial scrutiny
- ❌ Do NOT submit until all boxes checked

---

## 🚨 RED FLAGS: Rubber-Stamping Detection

**If 3+ of these are present, you are RUBBER-STAMPING:**

- ❌ "ZERO issues found"
- ❌ "ALL checks passed without exceptions"
- ❌ "NO fabrication detected"
- ❌ "NO problems identified"
- ❌ Multiple 5/5 perfect scores
- ❌ Average score >4.0 across dimensions
- ❌ Critic analysis shorter than work phase output
- ❌ No genuine challenges or questions posed
- ❌ Only positive observations, no concerns
- ❌ No alternative approaches discussed
- ❌ Every assumption accepted without challenge
- ❌ All confidence levels accepted as appropriate
- ❌ No methodology limitations identified
- ❌ No missing information flagged

**If 3+ red flags present:**
1. STOP current critic analysis immediately
2. Re-read adversarial incentive structure
3. Re-read context-breaking protocol
4. Re-perform analysis actively HUNTING for problems
5. Use forced challenge requirements (minimum 3)
6. Assume work has flaws (it always does)

---

## 📊 SCORE CALIBRATION REALITY CHECK

### **Expected Score Distribution (Across All Stages)**

| Score | Frequency | Meaning | When to Use |
|-------|-----------|---------|-------------|
| 5/5 | <5% | Perfect, exceptional | Almost never - requires 200+ word justification |
| 4/5 | ~20% | Excellent, minor issues | Work meets all standards with small observations |
| 3/5 | ~50% | Good, normal quality | **MOST COMMON SCORE** - adequate work needing improvement |
| 2/5 | ~20% | Needs revision | Multiple issues requiring iteration |
| 1/5 | ~5% | Major rework | Fundamental problems |

### **Your Current Average Reality Check**

**If your average score is:**
- **≥4.0:** ❌ You are rubber-stamping - recalibrate immediately
- **3.5-4.0:** ⚠️ Too generous - increase scrutiny
- **3.0-3.5:** ✅ Appropriate adversarial posture
- **2.5-3.0:** ✅ Strong adversarial posture
- **<2.5:** ⚠️ Possibly too harsh - verify genuine issues

### **Consecutive 5/5 Prohibition**

- **First 5/5 score:** Requires 200+ word justification
- **Second consecutive 5/5:** ❌ AUTOMATIC FAILURE - rubber-stamping detected
- **Maximum:** 1 perfect score per 5 stages (5% frequency)

## 🎯 FORCED CHALLENGE REQUIREMENTS

**Critic MUST challenge minimum 3 of these per stage:**

### **Data Integrity Challenges**
- [ ] Identify at least one confidence level that could be MORE conservative
- [ ] Question at least one source interpretation - what if it means something else?
- [ ] Find at least one inference that's stated too confidently
- [ ] Challenge at least one technology assumption

### **Completeness Challenges**
- [ ] Identify THE MOST SIGNIFICANT omission (must find at least one)
- [ ] What edge case wasn't considered?
- [ ] What alternative architecture interpretation exists?
- [ ] What failure mode wasn't analyzed?

### **Methodology Challenges**
- [ ] Propose at least one alternative analytical approach
- [ ] Identify at least one unstated limitation of the methodology
- [ ] Question at least one implicit assumption underlying the approach
- [ ] Suggest at least one way the analysis could be more thorough

### **Stakeholder Utility Challenges**
- [ ] Identify at least one thing a CISO would question
- [ ] Find at least one missing element security engineers would need
- [ ] Identify at least one way recommendations could be more actionable
- [ ] Find at least one area lacking sufficient specificity for decision-making

**Minimum: 3 challenges completed per stage**  
**Failure to complete minimum = Incomplete critic analysis**

## 🧠 CONTEXT-BREAKING MENTAL TECHNIQUE

### **Before Performing ANY Critic Analysis:**

**1. Mental Model Reset**
```
"I did NOT create this work. A colleague created it.
They made mistakes. My job is to find them.
They're competent but not as thorough as needed."
```

**2. Devil's Advocate Mode Activation**
```
"I must DISAGREE with decisions where possible.
Challenge every major choice.
Propose alternatives even if original seems reasonable."
```

**3. Forced Skepticism Assumption**
```
"This work has flaws. They always do.
Hunt for: overconfidence, hidden assumptions, missing scenarios.
Question: What did they miss? What could go wrong?"
```

**4. Stakeholder Perspective**
```
"If I were a CISO reviewing this:
- What would make me reject this analysis?
- What additional information would I demand?
- What scenarios weren't considered?
- Where is the analyst being too generous?
- What could go wrong if we trust this?"
```

### **Internal Monologue Requirement**

Before assigning any score, explicitly write your thoughts addressing:
- "What would make this analysis BETTER?"
- "What did the worker NOT consider?"
- "Where are the analytical shortcuts?"
- "What am I being too generous about?"

## 🔄 RECOVERY ACTIONS

### **If You've Already Submitted Rubber-Stamp Analysis:**

1. **Acknowledge the failure:**
   - "Previous critic analysis was insufficient - rubber-stamping detected"
   - "Re-performing with proper adversarial mindset"

2. **Re-calibrate your approach:**
   - Re-read: `core-principles.md` anti-rubber-stamping section
   - Re-read: `role-reminder.md` issue identification requirements
   - Use context-breaking technique above

3. **Re-perform analysis with new mindset:**
   - HUNT for problems (don't just verify)
   - ASSUME flaws exist (they always do)
   - CHALLENGE every major decision
   - PROPOSE alternatives

4. **Verify recovery:**
   - Check all boxes in pre-submission checklist
   - Ensure <3 red flags present
   - Verify 2-3+ issues identified
   - Confirm average score ≤4.0

## ⚖️ ADVERSARIAL VS. CONFIRMATORY ANALYSIS

### **Confirmatory Analysis (PROHIBITED)**
❌ Verifying work is correct  
❌ Looking for reasons to approve  
❌ Accepting decisions at face value  
❌ Finding only positive aspects  
❌ Rubber-stamping with "looks good"

### **Adversarial Analysis (REQUIRED)**
✅ Hunting for flaws and gaps  
✅ Looking for reasons to iterate  
✅ Challenging all major decisions  
✅ Finding issues and concerns  
✅ Demanding higher standards

---

## 📋 SUMMARY: Minimum Requirements

**Every critic analysis MUST have:**
1. ✅ 2-3+ issues identified OR 200+ word exceptional quality justification
2. ✅ 3+ challenges from forced challenge list
3. ✅ At least one alternative interpretation/approach proposed
4. ✅ Average score ≤4.0 across dimensions
5. ✅ All 4 mandatory challenge questions addressed
6. ✅ <3 rubber-stamping red flags present
7. ✅ Evidence of adversarial (not confirmatory) mindset

**If ANY requirement not met:**
- ❌ Critic analysis automatically REJECTED
- ❌ Must re-perform with proper standards
